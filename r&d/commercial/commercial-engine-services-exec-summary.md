# Commercial Engine & Services — Threat Intel Executive Summary

> C3PO Intel | 2026-04-23 | TLP: WHITE

The commercial engine and services sector is facing a 600% year-over-year surge in cyberattacks, with 27 major ransomware incidents recorded across aviation between January 2024 and April 2025. State-sponsored actors from China, North Korea, Iran, and Russia are running simultaneous, persistent campaigns targeting propulsion IP, MRO operations, and supply chain access.

China-nexus groups — primarily APT41 and Volt Typhoon — pose the highest sustained threat, actively exploiting VPN vulnerabilities to penetrate OT environments and tier 2/3 suppliers, with the strategic goal of compressing their domestic propulsion development timeline by stealing Western R&D. North Korea's Lazarus Group is running AI-augmented LinkedIn social engineering campaigns directly targeting aerospace engineers. Ransomware groups including LockBit, Cl0p, and Akira are layering financial extortion on top of espionage operations.

The most dangerous emerging risk is **data manipulation inside MRO environments** — attackers altering torque specifications, calibration settings, or diagnostic sensor feeds in ways that convert a cyber incident into an airworthiness event. 68% of OT intrusions now achieve system access within 24 hours.

**Immediate priorities:** Patch VPN endpoints, segment engine test cell OT, enforce time-limited vendor remote access, brief engineering staff on LinkedIn-based social engineering, and begin requiring SBOMs from software-embedded component suppliers.
