## Overview

The Com is not a group you infiltrate or arrest your way out of. It's a **criminal subculture** — a loosely connected online ecosystem of overlapping networks whose members share infrastructure, skills, status, and increasingly, victims. The FBI officially warned the public about it on **July 23, 2025**, describing it as "an international online ecosystem comprised of multiple interconnected networks."

What makes The Com distinct from traditional cybercrime is its **youth-pipeline model**: older members identify and groom younger participants — sometimes as young as 11 — on gaming platforms, Discord, and Telegram. What begins as bragging about hacking a Minecraft server escalates into SIM swapping, corporate extortion, and in some cases, real-world violence. The ecosystem functions simultaneously as a criminal enterprise, a status competition, and a radicalization pipeline.

The crimes that come out of The Com range from corporate ransomware attacks costing hundreds of millions of dollars all the way to child exploitation, animal abuse, and murder-for-hire. These aren't separate worlds — the same infrastructure, social networks, and sometimes the same people connect them.

## How The Com Recruits

The pipeline is remarkably consistent across cases:

1. **Contact** — members approach targets on Discord, Telegram, Roblox, Minecraft, Twitch, Steam
2. **Relationship building** — friendly, sometimes romantic interaction to build trust
3. **Escalating involvement** — minor criminal acts framed as pranks or games (account takeovers, SIM swapping)
4. **Status and belonging** — notoriety within The Com is a primary social currency; successful attacks are livestreamed and shared
5. **Deepening commitment** — members cross-pollinate between branches; a hacker might fund IRL violence; a sextortion victim becomes a recruit

> *"What starts as a prank or game cheat can evolve into sophisticated attacks seen from groups like Scattered Spider and ShinyHunters."* — Aiden Synott, researcher

The FBI noted that The Com **deliberately targets minors** in the U.S. because they face lighter criminal penalties than adults.

## Historical Evolution & Forum Lineage

The Com's infrastructure has evolved through a series of English-language criminal forums:

| Era | Platform | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ~2015 | **Dark0de** | Early English-speaking hacker marketplace; seized by FBI/Europol 2015 |
| 2015–2022 | **RaidForums** | 530,000+ users; data breach sales, SIM swap culture; founder Diogo Coelho arrested Jan 2022 |
| 2022–2023 | **BreachForums v1** | Successor to RaidForums; admin Conor Fitzpatrick arrested March 2023 |
| 2023–2024 | **BreachForums v2/v3** | Operated by ShinyHunters + Baphomet; seized May 2024 |
| 2025 | **BreachForums v4** | Relaunched by ShinyHunters; ShinyHunters arrested June 2025; offline August 2025 |
| 2026 | **Post-BreachForums** | Members migrated; fusion with Russian-language forums (Exploit.in, XSS) accelerating |
| Ongoing | **Telegram channels** | "The Comm Leaks" and derivative channels; primary real-time coordination layer |

**The Migration Effect:** Each law enforcement takedown dispersed members who hybridized with other ecosystems. The 2025 BreachForums shutdown has accelerated **fusion with Russian-speaking eCrime forums** — English-speaking Com callers are now being hired on platforms like Exploit.in by Eastern European ransomware operators for initial access via vishing. This is a meaningful capability escalation.

## Intel Priorities for This Repo

1. **Track Hacker Com subgroup emergence** — new crews will continue to spin out of the ecosystem; early profiling is high value
2. **Monitor vishing service advertising** on Exploit.in / XSS — English-speaking Com callers being offered to Russian RaaS affiliates represents a new fusion threat
3. **Forum succession watch** — BreachForums is offline but the pattern of relaunch is established; a successor clearnet forum is likely
4. **Swatting as intelligence signal** — when execs at a breached org report false emergency service contact, that's a high-confidence Snarky Spider / IRL Com indicator
5. **Minors as vectors AND victims** — The Com's deliberate recruitment of under-18s has legal and operational implications for incident response
